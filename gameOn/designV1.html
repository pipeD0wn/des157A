Overview

This page is a journey through my design process for the “Pumpkin Growing Contest” game. Players have two in-game weeks (about two minutes) to grow the biggest pumpkin using choices like watering, fertilizing, and avoiding pests.

Below are my thumbnail sketches, full-size sketch with interaction notes, and a digital design composition that explores my final vision for this game.

Thumbnail Sketches

I started with quick pencil thumbnails to explore different layouts for the timer, player gardens, and action buttons. These helped me decide on a split layout with P1 and P2 gardens and a clear callout area for the contest description.

Full-Size Sketch & Interactions

From the thumbnails, I chose one direction and sketched it larger. Here I mapped out the main interactions: clicking buttons to water or fertilize, a pest warning, and a day counter that advances over the two-week game period.

Digital Design Comp

Finally, I translated the sketch into a digital comp using a fall color palette and simple vector pumpkins. This version stays within a 1200 × 750 viewport, keeps key elements above the fold, and begins to establish the typography and visual style I will use when coding the game.
